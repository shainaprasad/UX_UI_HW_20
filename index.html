Student Experience Research
Role: Lead UX Researcher
Overview

Higher education institutions have a few key questions they need answered for optimal student success, such as: how do we improve academic intervention? how do we better support our students of color? how do we provide more financial resources to low-income attendees? how can we increase graduation rates and retention? what needs to be changed to improve overall university experience?

That’s where the Senior Exit Survey comes in, administered annually from January 1st through December 31st, it explores students’ academic and co-curricular experiences at San Francisco State University. The study is conducted on an ongoing basis to undergraduate students who are seniors intending to graduate within the calendar year to gain intel on their overall experience, close administrator knowledge gaps, and make university-wide changes.

Survey Items

Overall Experience
Satisfaction with Major Program
Involvement in Academic, Campus, and Community Activities
Improvement in Intellectual Abilities
Personal and Social Development
Post-Graduation Plans

Methods

Survey via Qualtrics (question style: Likert-scale, matrix, open-ended, demographic, multiple choice)
Generative Research
Evaluative Research
Competitor Analyses
Quantitative Analysis (using SQL & R)

Participants

755 students who received degree in 2021 (10% of graduating class)
Accessed target audience via incentives ($$ + prizes), email blasts, graduation registration check-list item

Collaborators
Department Head
Provost/Dean
Cross-Functional Stakeholders
Researchers
Data Analysts/Engineers
Process

The Senior Exit Survey is adapted annually based on student groups and needs at the time. The survey is analyzed in the first quarter of the following year using SQL and R to cross reference student data (student ID, demographics, major, etc.) with survey responses. Text analyses are conducted for open-ended responses as well. Next, key pain points are identified and a 5-year trend report is populated. These reports are then shared with key stakeholders such as provosts, deans, professors, and program directors to make equitable improvements campus-wide through direct student input.

Outcome

Discovered overall high student satisfaction (above 80%)
Identified primary improvement areas (course availability, COVID-19, and advising/infrastructure issues)
Closed administrator knowledge gaps
Made tangible campus recommendations (provide better financial literacy options and increase course offerings)
Improved graduation rates

Reflection

2022 survey completion rose by 49% from 2021, which is optimistic for newly implemented outreach efforts. The study could be expanded to a wider audience, such as parents and instructors, for a more comprehensive understanding of overall campus experience. Conducting focus groups to cross-reference with quantitative data would provide more accurate and exhaustive feedback. Outreach strategies could be improved for even greater participation.

Challenges/Limitations

Some challenges include lack of diversity in participant pool, the survey period not being aligned with the academic year, and restricted access to resources for more comprehensive study. There was no way to control for COVID-19 with its significant negative impact on student experience.
